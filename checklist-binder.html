Visa Binder Layout

Document organisation for medical, doc check & interview
londonvisatracker.github.io
v1.4 · Apr 2026
You need two trips to London: one for the medical + document check, one for the interview. A well-organised binder means the document check window runs smoothly and you aren't scrambling on interview day. Staples (binder) + Amazon (coloured dividers) is a popular combination.

Accordion folder — good for medical day organisation

Ring binder with labelled dividers — Medical, Doc Check, Petitioner, Joint Sponsor, B&Bean, US Domicile, Rev Visas, USCIS Notices

⭐ Front section — immediate access
Medical · Doc check · Interview
  • Passport
  • Interview Letter
  • DS-260 Confirmation Page
  • Completed Medical Questionnaire (IV18a)
  • Courier Registration Confirmation with QR Code
  • Pink Medical Slip (add after medical)
  • Sealed Embassy Packet (add after document check — do NOT open)
Section 1 — Medical documents
Medical + doc check
  • GP Summary / Patient Care Record Dated within 2–3 weeks of medical
  • Vaccination Records
  • ACRO Police Certificate Photo page facing front
  • Medical Reports If applicable: chronic conditions, mental health, TB/STI
  • Child Health Record / Red Book If applicable
Section 2 — Civil documents (originals + copies)
Doc check + interview backup
  • Birth Certificate Long form with parent names
  • Marriage Certificate
  • Divorce Decrees If applicable — all prior marriages
  • Death Certificates If applicable — former spouses
  • Adoption Documents If applicable
  • Name Change Documents If applicable
  • Court / Criminal Records If applicable
  • Military Records If applicable
  • Additional Police Certificates Non-UK, per reciprocity rules
Section 3 — Financial documents (copies)
Interview backup only

All financial documents must already be uploaded to CEAC. The officer will not accept new documents at the interview window.

  • Form I-864 — Petitioner Wet signed — no digital signatures
  • Form I-864 — Joint Sponsor If applicable
  • IRS Tax Transcripts
  • W-2s / 1099s
  • Employment Letter
  • Recent Pay Stubs
  • Proof of Status — Sponsor / Joint Sponsor US passport or green card
  • Proof of US Domicile If petitioner lives abroad
Section 4 — Identity & travel documents
Interview backup
  • Passport Biographic Page Copy
  • Previous Passports If relevant to travel/visa history
  • Visa History If applicable
  • USCIS Approval Notice (I-130 NOA2)
Section 5 — Supporting / case-specific
As needed
  • Immigration History Documents
  • Any documents specific to complex case history
Section 6 — Optional (rarely requested)
Backup only
  • Relationship Evidence Photos, messages, travel records
  • Additional Supporting Evidence

Which sections for each appointment

MedicalFront section + Section 1
Document checkFront section + Section 1 + Section 2
InterviewFront section primarily — Sections 2–4 as backup if asked
