⚠️ The I-864 MUST be wet signed. Print the completed form, sign by hand, scan it back in. Do NOT use a digital or e-signature — this is a common reason for delays. The I-864EZ is available for simple cases but many users encounter issues with it; the standard I-864 is recommended for all.

Recommendation: use the standard I-864 regardless of how simple your case appears. Many users experience problems with the EZ form even in straightforward situations.

Part 5 — Employment and income
Documents to attach: IRS tax transcript (most recent year minimum — get from IRS website, not a copy of your return). Adding W-2s, 6 months of pay stubs, and up to 3 years of transcripts is advised even if not explicitly required by NVC.
Part 6 — Assets (only if income is below threshold)
London-specific warning: The US Embassy London has demonstrated reluctance to accept assets-only cases. Cases relying primarily on savings, property equity, or investments have received 221(g) even when technically meeting thresholds. If income is close to or below the requirement — secure a joint sponsor. Do not rely on assets alone.
Part 7 — Sponsor's obligations (read before signing)

By signing the I-864, you agree to financially support the immigrant at 125% of the US poverty line (or 100% if on active duty sponsoring a spouse or child). This obligation is legally enforceable and lasts until the immigrant:

  • Becomes a US citizen, OR
  • Has worked 40 qualifying Social Security quarters (~10 years), OR
  • Loses permanent resident status and departs the US, OR
  • Dies.
Divorce does NOT end the I-864 obligation. The sponsor remains financially responsible regardless of the status of the relationship.
Joint Sponsor — if petitioner income is insufficient
Adding a joint sponsor causes a 2–3 day CEAC lockout while the system updates. Plan ahead and do not add a joint sponsor at the last minute.

The joint sponsor must be a US citizen or green card holder and must meet the income threshold independently based on their own household size. They complete a separate I-864 form.

  • Separate I-864 (wet signed)
  • IRS Tax Transcript (most recent year)
  • W-2s / 1099s
  • Proof of US status (passport or green card)
  • Proof of US domicile
  • W-2 employees: employment letter + recent pay stubs
  • Self-employed: 12 months of business bank statements + evidence of business
  • Retired: evidence of retirement income source (pension statements, SSA award letter)
